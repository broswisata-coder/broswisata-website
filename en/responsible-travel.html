Our field rules for Bukit Lawang orangutans and Tangkahan elephants.

Responsible travel changes how a route is paced, who guides it, what guests are told before an activity, and how much pressure we place on wildlife and its habitat.

01

No feeding wildlife

Guests are briefed not to feed, touch, call, chase, or crowd orangutans, elephants, or other wildlife.

02

Keep a safe distance

The guide controls spacing and pace. If an area is busy, the route can wait or adjust.

03

Use local guides

Jungle routes are arranged with local partners who understand trails, weather, forest conditions, and guest safety.

04

Tangkahan: observe, do not handle

We do not offer or promise elephant riding, feeding, bathing, touching, or close-contact encounters. Viewing is kept at a respectful distance.

How this changes the guest experience.

Responsible does not mean less memorable. It means the trip is calmer, more respectful, and easier to trust.

Before trekking

Guests get simple ground rules: no feeding, keep a safe distance, follow local guide instructions, avoid direct wildlife contact, and keep noise low.

During the route

The route can adjust to weather, trail conditions, guest fitness, and wildlife activity. In Tangkahan, elephant viewing remains distant and non-intrusive.

Local impact

Where possible, routes use local lodges, village meals, and partners who keep money closer to the destination.

After the trip

Guests are encouraged to share realistic feedback, not staged wildlife promises.

A good eco tour does not promise perfect wildlife moments. It promises careful planning, local respect, and honest expectations.

Responsible travel questions

The same principles apply in Bukit Lawang and Tangkahan: honest expectations, safe distance, local guide direction, and no forced wildlife interaction.

Can you guarantee orangutan sightings?

No responsible operator should guarantee wildlife. We can plan the right area, timing, guide partner, and trekking style.

Is Bukit Lawang suitable for families?

Often yes, if pacing and trek difficulty are matched to the group.

Do you support local communities?

We prioritize local guide partners, local lodges, village food stops, and local route support.

Does BROS Wisata offer elephant activities in Tangkahan?

We only plan respectful, distance-based viewing. We do not offer or promise elephant riding, feeding, bathing, touching, or close-contact encounters.
